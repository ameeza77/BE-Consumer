Study Notes

Introduction to Business and Society

Business does not operate alone. Every business exists within society and depends on people, resources, communities, laws, technology, and the environment.

A responsible business should not only ask "How much profit can we make?" — it should also ask "How will our decision affect consumers, society, and the environment?"

Meaning of Business and Society

Simple definition: Business and society refers to how business activities affect people, communities, and the environment.

Example: A food company may produce affordable meals — good for society. But poor-quality ingredients, misleading info, or excessive plastic waste can negatively affect society.

Why Business Responsibility Matters

A stakeholder is any person or group affected by business decisions — consumers, employees, suppliers, community, government, and the environment.

Responsibility builds trust, protects reputation, reduces risk, supports sustainability, improves loyalty, and helps long-term success.

Consumers

Consumers are people who buy and use products or services. Because consumers may not always have complete knowledge about a product, businesses must provide accurate information and treat consumers fairly.

Consumer Needs

Safety, quality, honest information, fair price, good service, privacy, and complaint solution — consumer needs go beyond just what to buy; they include fairness, respect, and protection.

Ethical Responsibilities Toward Consumers

1. Providing safe products   2. Giving honest information   3. Fair pricing   4. Respecting consumer privacy   5. Handling complaints fairly.

The Environment

The environment refers to natural surroundings affected by business activities — air, water, land, plants, animals, and natural resources.

Business Impact on the Environment

Negative: pollution, waste, resource depletion, deforestation, carbon emission.

Positive: recycling, energy saving, sustainable sourcing, green product design, environmental awareness.

Environmental Responsibility in Business

It does not mean a business must stop making profit — it means the business should make profit responsibly: reduce waste, save energy, reduce pollution, use sustainable materials, educate customers, follow environmental law.

Link Between Consumers and Environment

Consumers increasingly expect businesses to be safe, honest, fair, and environmentally responsible. Businesses must balance customer needs, environmental responsibility, cost, and profit.

Activity Task

Case Study: The Café and Plastic Waste

A café sells drinks in plastic cups, straws, and bags. It's popular among students because drinks are cheap, but rubbish bins outside are always full of plastic waste. The owner worries eco-friendly packaging will raise costs.

  1. What environmental issue is shown in this case?
  2. What consumer need is involved?
  3. How can the café balance price and environmental responsibility?
  4. Suggest one practical action the café can take.
